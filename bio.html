Biography

Lenka Zviadadze (professionally known as Lenka Z) was born on September 6, 2001, in Tbilisi, Georgia. She made her first television appearance at the age of three on the “Imedi” channel. That same year, she won the children’s competition Little Miss Caucasus, earning titles such as Mini Miss Princess and Star of the Future. At four, Lenka received a diploma titled “For Devotion to the Song” from the musical studio Sisters Chokhonelidze, while also beginning her modeling career with NOVA, appearing in fashion shows and commercials.

At five, she was crowned Georgian Miss Charm at the “Little Miss Georgia” beauty contest. By age six, she joined the ballet studio under the Vakhtang Chabukiani State School of Ballet, directed by internationally acclaimed ballerina Nino Ananiashvili. That same year, she competed in the children’s musical contest Ana Bana on the Rustavi 2 television channel.

At seven, Lenka began attending the Z. Paliashvili Music School for Gifted Children, where she continued her training as both a vocalist and pianist. She went on to win several youth festivals, including Merry Notes, 5 VEGA, and in 2009, the Chopin Festival. By the age of ten, she became a finalist on the megaproject Got Talent (Georgia’s Got Talent) on Rustavi 2.

In 2012, Lenka was invited by French director Redha Benteifour to perform one of the lead roles in the musical Melodies of Vera District. She shared the stage with iconic figures including Vakhtang Kikabidze, Sofia Nizharadze, and the choreographic ensemble Sukhishvili. She also performed at receptions hosted by Catholicos-Patriarch Ilia II and appeared in concerts with artists such as Nani Bregvadze, Nino Katamadze, and Mamuka Charkviani. Lenka performed as both singer and pianist with the Tbilisi Opera and Ballet Theatre Orchestra, and later won a competition at the Art Academy of Classical Music.

In 2013, she won the international music competition Slavianski Bazaar in Vitebsk, and in 2014 she became a finalist of the internationally broadcast competition New Wave. That same year, she won the online contest of the Georgian TV show Bravo.

After a short break, Lenka returned in 2018 by winning the SULIKO International Children’s and Youth Music Festival. She also participated in Jessie J’s Rose Challenge, writing original lyrics to Jessie J’s instrumental track. Jessie J publicly recognized her work and shared it on social media.

From 2018 to 2019, Lenka toured nationally and internationally with Georgian artist Mamuka Charkviani. In 2021, she competed on The Voice of Georgia and earned a four chair turn for her audition.

In 2022, Lenka moved to New York City to pursue a BFA in Theatre at Adelphi University. She made her American stage debut as Helen of Troy in Ellen McLaughlin’s adaptation of The Trojan Women, marking a new chapter in her artistic journey.

Lenka continues to develop as a singer, actor, and creator, blending her classical foundation with contemporary performance, cinematic storytelling, and cross-cultural artistic expression.
